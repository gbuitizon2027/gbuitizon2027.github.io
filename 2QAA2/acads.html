Core Courses

Professor Aurora Sinistra

Pique your curiosities about the cosmos and begin to explain everything that we observe in the universe. Map out constellations, observe faraway galaxies using telescopes, learn more about different celestial bodies. All that and more in our astronomy course.

Professor Filius Flitwick

Fancy yourself to a prancing cupcake? You're in luck. Here you will learn the appropriate hand gestures and proper pronunciation to cast charms such as Levitation and Fire-Making. Ready your wand, books, parchment and quill as things are going to get interesting.

Professor Remus Lupin

Dark creatures? Hexes? Fear them no more as you learn to combat these using various offensive and defensive spells. From the small yet terrible Cornish Pixies to the fearsome trolls, counter them with spells such as Petrificus Totalus and Peskipiksi Pesternomi.

Professor Rolanda Hooch

Soar bravely through the skies! Take your Broom Flight Class and learn the basics. Summoning, mounting, lift-off, standing, and much more. Try and test your luck in applying for the Quidditch teams, see you soon.

Professor Pomona Sprout

Study the magical and mundane, sometimes dangerous, plants and fungi. Learn about their anatomy, magical properties, utilization and proper care and handling. Visit the seven greenhouses on campus if you get the chance, but be mindful of unearthed mandrakes and devil's snare.

Professor Severus Snape

Learn how to bottle fame, brew glory, and even put a stopper to death. You will be provided with a potions kit containing ingredients like belladonnas and equipment such as books, glass phials, a weighing scale, and a standard cauldron to be used in every class. Make sure to pay close attention to the intricacies of timing, ageing, stirring techniques, and bottling.

Professor Minerva McGonagall

The art of changing the form and appearance of an object or a person. Though often said to be complex and dangerous is not always the case if studied properly. Learn the basic transfiguration alphabet and formulas which are directly influenced by the subject's bodyweight, viciousness, wand power, and concentration. A happy transfiguration to all.

Grading System

Passing Grades ✓

  • O

    Outstanding Always continues to N.E.W.T.

  • E

    Exceeds Expectations Usually continues to N.E.W.T.

  • A

    Acceptable Rarely continues to N.E.W.T.

Failing Grades ☓

  • P

    Poor May repeat subject

  • D

    Dreadful May not receive O.W.L. credit

  • T

    Troll Fail with distinction. More than one T may mean refusal into other N.E.W.T.s

End of Year Examinations

Exams

For 1st-4th and 6th years

All students at the first, second, third, fourth, and sixth year levels are required to complete a set of examinations to get into the next year of schooling.

There is one exam for each subject.

These exams are Hogwarts-regulated, but can range from purely written tests to practical ones.

O.W.L.s

For 5th years

Ordinary Wizarding Levels, alternatively known as O.W.L.s, are a set of standardized, subject-specific tests for fifth year students, administered by the Wizarding Examinations Authority.

The O.W.L.s are some of the most important exams a student can take, as they determine what course or specialty a student could continue to study in their final years at Hogwarts.

O.W.L.s are in the form of both written and practical examinations, taken over a two-week period.

N.E.W.T.s

For 7th years

The N.E.W.T.s are the entrance exams in order to get into optional levels of education for exceptional students.

Courses that are in the N.E.W.T.-level have increasingly difficult subject matter.

In order to progress to N.E.W.T., a student had to score either an 'Outstanding' or an 'Exceeds Expectations' on their O.W.L.
